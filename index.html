My Top 5 Cricketers

By Bala Nair

In India, Cricket isn't just a game. It is a bona fide religion. A religion where cricketers are Gods and every victory is a festival.Indians just need an excuse to celebrate. Remember the epic party that followed after we won the 2011 World Cup?

Sachin Tendulkar

In a nation of 1.3 billion people with 1.3 billion problems, he was and still is a source of pride and joy to us all. I grew up watching him play and win so many matches for India. He is the perfect role model for any budding sportperson. There is nothing more nostalgic than Harsha Bhogle talking about "the first ever 200 runs in ODI cricket" .

Rahul Dravid

Rahul Dravid was probably one of the last classical Test match batsmen.His progress into the national side may have been steady and methodical rather than meteoric, but once there, Dravid established himself at the vanguard of a new, defiant generation that were no longer easybeats away from home. His "Century at Lords" in 2011 is my all time favorite.

AB de Villiers

There is hardly an Indian cricket fan these days who does not love AB de Villiers.The Indian fans worship De Villiers to the extent that they have even cheered him on during India-South Africa encounters. If there is any batsmen who can "smash any bowler" in the world,it's definitely AB de Villiers.

Wassim Akram

Wassim Akram is to me the best ever fast bowler the world has ever seen. With a short runup and ability to "swing the ball" both ways, he was a nightmare more most accopmplished batsmen in the world.He used to play for Pakistan which was the team that Indians hated the most.Inspite of that i still like him for his sheer cricketing abilities.

Anil Kumble

Anil Kumble is one of the two crickets to have claimed all 10 wickets in an innings. He is the highest wicket taker in test cricket for India till date. I also have a personal fond memory of having met him in person at a function in bangalore. He is gusty player and no one can forget how he bowled in West Indies with a "broken jaw".
